Bass Fishing Lure Guide by Season and Clarity

Time of Year Water Temp Water Clarity Bass Behavior Recommended Lures Notes
Early Spring (Mar–Apr) 45–58°F Clear Pre-spawn, holding on deeper edges
  • Suspending jerkbait (natural shad)
  • Finesse jig
 Fish are sluggish but may suspend over points or breaks.
Slightly Muddy Pre-spawn, staging near structure
  • Red lipless crankbait
  • Chartreuse spinnerbait
 Use reaction baits and target wood or rock transitions.
Muddy Pre-spawn, tight to shallow cover
  • Black/blue jig with pork trailer
  • Rattling squarebill crankbait
 Fish slow, bulky presentations with vibration near shoreline cover.
Mid to Late Spring (Apr–May) 55–68°F Clear Spawning; beds visible
  • Weightless soft plastics (green pumpkin)
  • Floating worm
 Target bedding fish carefully. Light line and finesse presentations work best.
Slightly Muddy Spawning; relating to visible and cover-based beds
  • Texas-rigged creature bait
  • Chartreuse-tipped Senko
 Use bright accents or noise to help fish locate lures in cloudier water.
Muddy Spawning close to banks with cover
  • Black/blue creature bait
  • Short-arm spinnerbait
 Flip and pitch directly to cover, keeping lures in place longer.
Early Summer (June) 68–75°F Clear Post-spawn, roaming drop-offs
  • Topwater walking bait
  • Carolina rig with finesse worm
 Great for targeting transition zones and early morning schooling.
Slightly Muddy Post-spawn, suspended over cover
  • Swim jig with trailer
  • Buzzbait near shorelines
 Focus on horizontal presentations with noise or vibration.
Muddy Post-spawn, holding to isolated structure
  • Spinnerbait with Colorado blades
  • Dark-colored flipping jig
 Stick close to visible structure. Strike zone is very small.
Mid to Late Summer (Jul–Aug) 75–85°F Clear Deep structure, thermocline present
  • Drop shot (morning dawn)
  • Deep-diving crankbait
 Target offshore humps and ledges. Early/late bite best.
Slightly Muddy Moving deeper but still relating to cover
  • Football jig
  • Big Texas-rigged worm
 Use slow, bottom-dragging baits with scent and bulk.
Muddy Lethargic and tight to cover
  • Heavy spinnerbait
  • Black buzzbait at low light
 Focus on dusk/dawn; use noisy lures that push water.
Early Fall (Sept) 70–75°F Clear Chasing shad in open water
  • Topwater spook
  • Silver underspin
 Match the hatch with shad-colored baits near baitfish schools.
Slightly Muddy Feeding up before turnover
  • Squarebill crankbait
  • White spinnerbait
 Work shallow flats and backs of coves. Focus on baitfish activity.
Muddy Tight to wood and shallow brush
  • Black and blue chatterbait
  • Bright squarebill
 Fish aggressively. Bass use shallow cover as ambush points.
Winter (Dec–Feb) 38–45°F Clear Suspended deep and sluggish
  • Blade bait
  • Hair jig
 Vertical presentations over structure or deep bait balls.
Slightly Muddy Deep, but tighter to bottom or isolated cover
  • Jig and pork/minnow trailer
  • Slow-rolled spinnerbait
 Use scent and slow retrieve to trigger lethargic strikes.
Muddy Minimal movement, cover-oriented
  • Black/blue football jig
  • Compact Texas rig
 Keep bait in the strike zone as long as possible. Very slow retrieve.
